Tutoring "Software Engineering"

Diagramming, Software-Development Process, Quality & Management

My first tutoring job at the university of Lübeck was for the lecture "Software Engnieering" at the institute for Software Engineering and Programming Languages (ISP). I decided to apply for this specific course as I didn't dive as deep as I would've like to during the semester, thus giving me the opportunity to go through it again in even more detail. Now, being the student assistent, I am tutoring during the winter semester and supervise the hands-on software development in summer semesters, as this lecture is split into the theoretical and the practical part. The theoretical part (winter semesters) of the lecture goes into detail concerning software quality, modeling in softwaredevelopement, the phases a software project goes through, some design patterns and management in terms of softwaredevelopment. During the summer semesters students had to overengineer boardgames, including most diagramms introduced in the winter semester, writing tests and designing a GUI. As a tutor you get about 10 groups you supervise and help if necessary. All in all it has been a fun experience and a good practice to work with and supervise small groups of future software engineers.

Tutoring "Algorithms & Datastructures"

Fundamentals, Complexity & Estimation

The second tutoring job I landed was at the Institute of Information Systems (IFIS). I especially wanted to return back to my second semester's favorite to get an indepth recap of the things that really pathed my studies; The fundamental algorithms and basic datastructures, what makes them special and how exactly do they work. The topics this lecture covered surrounded various sorting algorithms, hashing, many treestructures and their algorithms such as dijkstra, dynamic programming and introduced the idea of complexity classes, for example P and NP. In contrast to my first tutoring in software engineering, this was a whole different setup to work in, because the focus was a more theoretical one. Explaining and discussing the complexity and benefits of certain algorithms compared to others really helped me improve my overall understanding of efficiency and computability

Tutoring "Developement of Safety-Critical Software"

Theorem Proving, Model Checking, Runtime Verification & Rust

My third and final tutoring job at the university of Lübeck was for the lecture "Development of Safety-Critical Software" at the institute for Software Engineering and Programming Languages (ISP) (again). I was given the opportunity to tutor as I graduated the course with the years highest grade and while achiving a good final grade, I was really unsatisfied with the way the given Information was presented. With a change in exercise management, I was consulted to review and revision the lectures slides and overall structure. Due to the very intense confrontation with the lectures topics I grew even more confident in safety critical software developement processes and got a good understanding of the methods and how they can be integrated in a common, day to day software engineering process.
